## Research Experience

**Design, modeling, and control of a hydraulically actuated robot** — Atlanta, United States  
*Project Leader, RoboMed Lab* (Oct 2024 – Present)  
*Skills: CAD Modeling, Robotics Modeling & Control*
- Develop and manufacture a polymer-based hydraulic robotic system for non-conductive, MRI safe environment.
- Formulate the physical model for the hydraulic robot and refine it with a data-driven hysteresis model.
- Implement position feedback for accurate control and compare the inverse model control with PID control.
- Conduct experiments to characterize and validate the system, and demonstrate it under a vascular model.

**Mechanism and actuation design of COAST Guidewire Robot** — Atlanta, United States  
*Project Assistant, RoboMed Lab* (Jul 2025 – Present)  
*Skills: Circuit & PCB Design*
- Contribute to a modified COAST robotic guidewire enabling active stiffness control to enhance steering and quick-connect usability in minimally invasive cardiovascular interventions. *(Paper prepared for IJRR)*
- Manage electrical development of the actuation system (PCB and circuit design) and develop an analog force sensor to enable precise robotic actuation and sensing.

**Navigation System for Surgeries** — Shenzhen, China  
*Project Leader, Brain-robot Rehabilitation Lab* (Feb 2023 – Jun 2024)  
*Skills: Software & Hardware Design*
- Co-designed hardware and software for a surgery navigation system, including circuit design to integrate micro-controller and sensor, C embedded programming, and real-time tracking feedback through Unity.
- Conducted experiments to validate effectiveness (validity and reliability) with an optical system and robot arm.

**Movement Analysis using IMU Sensors** — Stockholm, Sweden  
*Visiting Student, Intelligent Heart Technology Lab* (Oct 2023 – Jan 2024)  
*Skills: Kalman Filter & Sensor Fusion*
- Investigated relationship between movement and cardiovascular function through gait parameters and heart rate.
- Deployed a Kalman filter for orientation estimation, calibrated IMU angles, and extracted gait parameters and CoM.

**Motion Capture for Rehabilitation** — Shenzhen, China  
*Project Leader, Brain-robot Rehabilitation Lab* (Apr 2022 – May 2024)  
*Skills: Signal Processing, Deep Learning*
- Integrated UWB technology and IMU to develop a motion capture system for human motion recovery.
- Developed Unity visualization and Python signal transmission, and applied deep learning for reconstruction.
- Evaluated system effectiveness on healthy subjects during rehabilitation exercises.

**Center of Mass Estimation and Rehabilitation Applications** — Shenzhen, China  
*Project Assistant, Brain-robot Rehabilitation Lab* (Oct 2022 – Aug 2023)  
*Skills: Data Analysis, Acquisition*
- Utilized IMU-based motion capture and developed an OpenSim-based algorithm to estimate the 3D center of mass.
- Analyzed center of mass to extract motion features and perform rehabilitation assessments.
